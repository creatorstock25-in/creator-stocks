Invest in Creators. Earn When They Win.

Creator Stocks lets fans invest in their favorite creators’ next videos and share profits when the content succeeds. Creators raise funds — fans earn returns. Fair. Transparent. Built for creators and their communities.

How Creator Stocks Works

A simple, three-step funding model that aligns creators and fans. Creators list a video project and funding goal. Fans invest small amounts. When the video earns profit, investors receive their share.

1
Creators List Projects

Post your next video budget, select investors, and set per-investor limits.

2
Fans Invest

Fans choose a project and pledge small amounts — $10, $50, or $100.

3
Profits Are Shared

After about 4 weeks, 50% of the creator’s profit is shared with investors. Underperforming videos roll forward investor principal to future profits.

Why Creator Stocks

Creators shouldn’t have to choose between personal debt and chasing sponsors. Creator Stocks empowers creators to fund their ideas while giving fans a meaningful way to support and earn.

For Creators

  • Raise production funds without debt
  • Choose your number of investors
  • Focus on creation, not sponsorship hunting

For Investors

  • Back creators you love with micro-investments
  • Earn ROI when videos perform well
  • Protected principal — underperforming projects get repaid later

The Break-Even Promise

If a video underperforms, investments aren’t lost — they’re repaid from the creator’s next profitable videos. This keeps creators motivated and protects investor funds.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do investors get payouts?

Payouts are shared automatically after each video’s revenue cycle ends — typically 4 weeks after release.

What if a video flops?

Investor principal is protected: it’s repaid from the creator’s next profitable video.

Can I be both creator and investor?

Yes. You can fund your own projects and invest in others.

Is this real investing?

It’s a creator funding system built around performance-based revenue sharing, not traditional securities.
